## On AppFactory

The pitch is simple: developers can tokenize their repos. Your code backs a token. You earn fees when people trade it.

This sounds like it could be gimmicky. Another crypto thing.

But I've been sending these emails for days now, and I've come to understand why it matters.

Open source developers build incredible things and often get nothing for it. GitHub Sponsors helps, but it's charity. Consulting is trading time for money. VC funding means giving up control.

AppFactory offers something different: **ownership that scales.**

If your project gets popular, you benefit directly. Not through donations. Through a market that values what you built.

One developer made $300,000 in fees. That's not a typo.

I don't know if this will work at scale. But I know the problem is real, and this is a genuine attempt to solve it.
